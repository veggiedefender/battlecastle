Imaginext™ Battle Castle Help

Getting Started

This section covers everything you need to know to set up and start Imaginext™ Battle Castle on your computer.

For more information on this product's technical support, view the ReadMe file.

Windows Installation and Features

Insert the Imaginext Battle Castle CD into the CD-ROM drive and close the door. The following features will be available when the Autorun screen appears:

  • Install – Click on Install when the Autorun screen appears and follow the prompts to complete the installation. (See complete install instructions below.) Once the program has been installed, the Autorun button will read Play.
  • Imaginext™ Movie - Click here for a look at the Pirate Raider CD-ROM product and the Imaginext Lost Fortress toy.
  • Rescue Heroes™ Movie – Click here for a look at upcoming Rescue Heroes products.
  • Help – Click this button or click on Manual from the program group to access the manual.
  • Exit – If you wish to exit the Autorun screen, click this button.

Installing the Program

Imaginext Battle Castle features the AutoPlay function available on Windows. Insert the Imaginext Battle Castle CD into the CD-ROM drive and close the drive door. Click on Install when the Autorun screen appears and follow the prompts to complete the installation. Once the program has been installed, the Autorun button will read Play. Select this button to play the game.

If your system does not support AutoPlay, follow these steps to install Imaginext Battle Castle:

·         Click on Start to open the Start Menu.

·         Click on Run.

·         At the Open box, type "D:\install.exe". If your CD-ROM drive is a different letter than D, type that letter in place of D.

·         Click OK and follow the prompts.

Running the Program

To start the program using the AutoPlay feature of Windows, just insert the Imaginext Battle Castle CD into the CD-ROM drive and click Play when the Autorun screen appears. If your CD-ROM drive does not support AutoPlay, follow these steps to start Imaginext Battle Castle:

·         Close all running Windows applications.

·         Click on Start.

·         Move the cursor to Programs and select the Imaginext folder in your list of installed programs.

·         Move the cursor to the Imaginext Battle Castle folder and click on the Imaginext Battle Castle icon.

Uninstalling the Program

Follow these steps to use the uninstall function available under Windows to remove Imaginext Battle Castle from your system:

·         Click on Start.

·         Move the cursor to Settings.

·         Click on Control Panel.

·         Double-click on Add/Remove Programs.

·         Select Imaginext Battle Castle from the list.

·         Follow the prompts.

Macintosh Installation and Features

Insert the Imaginext Battle Castle CD into the CD-ROM drive and close the door.

Installing the Program

In order to run the program, you will first need to run the installer to set up the necessary program files on your hard drive. Double-click the Imaginext Battle Castle icon on the CD and follow the prompts to install these files.

Running the Program

To start the Macintosh version of Imaginext Battle Castle:

·         Insert the CD into your CD-ROM drive.

·         Close all running Macintosh applications; double-click on the Imaginext Battle Castle icon to show the contents of the CD.

·         Double-click on the Imaginext Battle Castle icon. If you have installed the Imaginext Battle Castle folder on your hard disk, double-click on the Imaginext Battle Castle icon in the folder located there.

Welcome to Imaginext™ Battle Castle!

Are you ready to rule the kingdom? Take the role of a knight as you travel the land in search of the power, experience and riches to build a grand castle and become king. Navigate the wide meadows, dark forests and narrow gorges of the land as you free victims from hideous goblins, liberate castles seized by invaders, and face off against competitive knights. It all leads to a final test: penetrate Dragomont's lair and imprison his dragon.

Along the way, you'll be able to take the spoils you earn from battle and use them to build the castle of your dreams. You'll craft your own adventure as you undertake a series of missions that will make you ever more powerful in your quest to vanquish the evil Dragomont and prove you're ready to rule the kingdom.

How Do I Sign In?

Before you can set out on your missions, you have to enter your name in the sign-in screen.

·        Use the keyboard to type your name. You can use a maximum of 14 letters, and up to 99 names can be entered.

·        If you’ve signed in previously, your name will appear on the list. You may have to use the arrows to scroll up and down to find it. Click on your name to continue.

·        After typing or selecting your name, click on the check mark at the bottom of the screen or press the Enter key on your keyboard to start your game.

·        To delete a name from the list, click on it and then click on the X button on the screen. You can also press Control+D on a PC or Option+D on a Macintosh.

The Mission Screen

Here you'll meet your squire who'll be your helpful guide for the entire game. On this screen your Squire tells you what you'll do on each of your missions. For each mission, you'll see visual representations of the goals you'll want to accomplish. For example, the first mission is to duel an enemy knight, so the Mission Screen displays an image of a knight.

You can return to the Mission Screen at any point during your mission for a reminder of what the mission goals are. When you've completed a mission, you'll want to return to the Mission Screen to see what your goals are for the next mission.

Once a mission is completed, you'll also have the ability to return to that mission later. This can provide a fun way to revisit your favorite missions at any time.

The Mission Screen is also the only location from which you can quit the game. To do so, just click the Exit button at the lower left of the screen.
       
       
       

The Toolbar

The on-screen toolbar appears during your adventures and has a number of buttons to help you on your missions:

The Exit Button – Click this button during navigation to go back to the Mission Screen.

The Stop Button – Click this button to bring your knight to a stop. Then the button will change and show an image of a running knight. If you click the button again, your knight will start moving again. You can also stop and restart your knight by pressing the Shift key on your keyboard.

The Movement Arrows – Click on the Left, Right, Up or Down buttons to move your knight. You can also move your knight by using the Left, Right, Up or Down arrow keys on your keyboard. The Jump button is in the middle of the directional buttons. While using the horse, you can jump over obstacles by pressing the Jump button. You can also jump by pressing the Spacebar on your keyboard.

The Castle Button – Click this button if you’d like to return to your castle to change vehicles or to make changes to your castle. Returning to your castle will also repair your armor.

The Scroll Button – Click this button to open your scroll and take a look at your current mission progress, your progress for the entire game, and any certificates you’ve earned.

During knight challenges, your toolbar changes slightly for use against enemy knights.

The Surrender Button – Click this button if you’d like to surrender in the middle of a challenge.

The Stop Button – This button functions the same as during navigation.

The Movement Arrows – These function the same as during navigation.

The Sword Button – Click this button to swing your sword at the enemy. You can also swing your sword by pressing the Spacebar on your keyboard.

The Sizzle Potion Button – Click this button to drop any sizzle potions you’ve obtained from the Wizard's Tower. You can also drop sizzle potions by pressing the Ctrl key on your keyboard. The dots surrounding the button let you know how many potions you have left. The Sizzle Potion button will stay gray (inactive) until you’ve gone to the Wizard's Tower and gotten some sizzle potions.

Navigating the World

As you set out on your first mission, you'll take control of your knight on foot. To turn your knight, use the Left and Right arrow keys on your keyboard. If you prefer to use the mouse, you can also click on the Left and Right arrow buttons on your toolbar.

You can stop or start movement by pressing the Shift key. You can also click the Stop button on your toolbar to stop your knight. The button will change to a running knight, and clicking the button again will resume your knight’s movement.

Each mission will have a different map to explore, so you'll want to travel around to see what kind of adventure you can find.

During navigation, your Knight’s shield can be seen at the top left of your screen. You can tell how strong your armor is and how much damage has been done to it, by the look of the shield and by the number on it. If the number on your knight’s shield reaches zero, you’ll automatically return to your castle where your armor will be fully repaired.

You’ll come to a number of places, objects, and events as you explore the kingdom in the various missions. These include:

Your Castle – This is where you can switch vehicles or make changes to your castle. You can also return there by clicking the Castle button on your toolbar.

Enemy Knights – These knights are your enemies, and bumping into them will result in the Knight Challenge game. As you go on more missions, the enemies will increase in power.

Enemy Castles – These castles have fallen into the hands of enemy ogres in the service of Dragomont. You can go to one to play the Castle Siege game.

Attacked Castles – These castles are under attack. You need to help defend them from the ogres. You can go to one to play the Castle Defense game.

Friendly Castles – These castle are in need of your help. If you bring the potion they need, they give you a reward.

Goblin Dungeons – These dungeons are controlled by evil goblins who've taken knights as prisoners. Go to one and you can play the Dungeon Rescue game.

Shacks – Be careful when you visit these shacks. Sometimes they add to your treasure, and sometimes they take from you.

Wizard's Towers – These structures will sell you powerful potions that you can use in the game.

Dragomont's Dragon – This creature has been set loose on the kingdom. If you get too close, he will do damage to your armor with his fiery breath. You can protect yourself from damage by getting the Dragon Brew Potion at the Wizard's Tower.

Treasure – These treasure bags can be found around the kingdom. You’ll want to explore and see whether you can find some.

Knight Challenges

Knight Challenges will happen when you bump into an enemy knight. You and the enemy knight (or knights) will go to the “dueling arena.”  Your toolbar will change slightly so that you can use some of the buttons during battle. Your armor strength is shown on your shield at the upper left of the screen, and your enemy's armor strength is shown on the shield at the upper right.

Pressing the Spacebar on the keyboard will swing your sword at your enemy. The duel continues until one knight has run out of armor points, shown on the shields. You can choose to surrender a duel by clicking the Surrender button on the toolbar.

Castle Sieges

You can choose to lay siege to a castle by navigating to it on the map. You’ll be taken to the Castle Siege game, where you’ll fire at enemy cannons as you try and avoid being fired on by them.

To move your cannon from side to side, just move your mouse. You can choose where to fire by moving your cursor around the screen. Position your cursor over one of the enemy cannons and click your mouse to fire at it.

Keep an eye out for any cannons moving into place, to make sure you fire on the them before they can fire at your cannon. If a castle cannon fires at your cannon before you’re able to fire, your cannon may take some damage.

The damage to your cannon will be shown on your shield at the upper left of the screen. As you take damage, the number of points your cannon has will decrease, getting closer to zero. In the same way, the damage to the castle can be seen on its shield at the upper right of the screen.

  
     

Castle Defenses

You can choose to defend a castle by navigating to it on the map. You’ll be taken to the Castle Defense game, where you’ll defend a castle from invading ogres. Your knight is atop the castle wall and runs back and forth trying to stop the ogres from climbing ladders onto the castle.

To move your knight along the castle wall, use the Left and Right arrow keys on your keyboard. To knock down a ladder, you can use either the Spacebar or the Up arrow key.

Be sure to knock down all the ladders before an ogre can climb up to the castle. If an ogre reaches the top before you knock him down, your knight's armor will take some damage.

The damage to your knight's armor will be shown on your shield at the upper left of the screen. As you take damage, the number of points your knight's armor has will decrease, getting closer to zero. In the same way, the damage to the ogre troops can be seen on their shield at the upper right of the screen.

  
     

Dungeon Rescues

A Dungeon Rescue happens when you go to a goblin's dungeon. This game involves a fast-paced dash through the dungeon as you try to free the prisoner held by the goblins. Along the way, try to collect as many bags of gold as you can. The goblins within the dungeon will try and stop you by rolling barrels in your direction and throwing fruit at you to tire you out. See if you can reach the end of the dungeon – and the prisoner held there – before you run out of energy.

Use your keyboard to make the following moves during a Dungeon Rescue:

Run Left – Use the Left arrow key.

Run Right – Use the Right arrow key.

Jump – Use the Up arrow key.

Duck and Roll – Use the Down arrow key.

Swing Your Sword – Use the Spacebar.

Climb Up a Ladder – Use the Up arrow key when you’re next to a ladder.

Climb Down a Ladder – Use the Down arrow key when you’re next to a ladder.

Your Castle

       

Your castle is where you can get a closer look at your knight and choose the vehicle that he explores in. You can go there by either clicking the Castle button on your toolbar, or by traveling there on the map.

You can click on the items your knight currently has, to hear information about them.

When you’ve gathered more vehicles and shields on your missions, you can go to your castle to choose which ones to use on your current mission. Click on any of the vehicles you've acquired to switch to it. The vehicle buttons are along the right side of the screen. You can also change your shield by clicking on a different one along the left side of the screen. Items you have not yet acquired will be grayed out.

The vehicles each have different features that can help in your quest to capture Dragomont and become king:

Knight – You start your adventures as a knight on foot.
Horse – With the horse, you will be able to jump across small chasms and walls.
Coach – The coach is a fearsome vehicle you can use to make enemy knights flee from you during missions.
Battle Wagon – The battle wagon has a drill that can break through rock. With the battle wagon, you can go through boulders, stone walls, and even mountains.
Flying Lion – With the flying lion, you can soar through the kingdom with ease. You can fly over mountains, lakes, boulders and trees. Most knights will be unable to reach you as well.

If you have a printer, you can print a picture of your knight in his current gear by clicking on the Print button.

Clicking on the Build Castle button takes you to the Castle Building activity, where you can create your own castle.
       

Castle Building

       

In Castle Building, you get to use the castle pieces you've earned in the game to make a castle of your own. There are four different castle designs (or templates) that you can build on. Click the Castle Template button to change templates.

The castle pieces are sorted into four groups: Walls, Towers, Rooms, and Props. To make a change to your castle, click on the Wall, Tower, Room, or Props button. The items you have earned can be seen in the inventory area at the bottom of the screen. A number next to each item shows you how many of them you have. Any items that you haven't earned will be grayed out.

When you click an item that you want to add to your castle, all the possible places you can put it will be highlighted. Drag the item to a location and click to place it. If a different item is already in that spot, it will be placed back in your inventory.

To remove an item, click the Remove Piece button. Your cursor will change into a cannon. Now click on any item in your castle to remove it. You can also remove all items from the castle by clicking the Remove All button. Removed items will be placed back in your inventory.

If you have a printer, you can print a picture of the castle you’re working on by clicking on the Print button.
       

Wizard's Tower

As you travel around the kingdom, you may run into a wizard who is willing to trade power-up potions for gold. If you have enough gold, just click on the item you want to get and then click on the Trade button. You can also choose to exit if you don’t want to spend your gold at the Wizard's Tower.

Power-ups

The Wizard's Tower has a variety of items to trade. Some potions are very rare and are not often seen. The potions include:

A potion that repairs damage to your armor.

A potion that increases your strength, letting you do more damage in a Knight Challenge.

A potion that makes you unbeatable. Enemies can do no damage to your knight.

A potion that makes you invisible to your enemies.

Sizzle drops that you can use during a Knight Challenge to damage your enemy’s armor.

A "Dragon’s Brew" that calms the dragon and allows you to pass safely.

A special potion that lets you change a boulder into gold. This is only found by exploring the map.

Quest Potions can be found during special missions. Trade these potions at friendly castles to receive a reward.

Scroll

The scroll can be seen by using the Scroll button on the toolbar. When you click the button, you’ll open the first page of the scroll, which contains information on your current mission. You can see what the goals are for your current mission, as well as how much gold you have.

 

Click the second tab on your scroll to see your progress since you began playing the game. You can see statistics on all the action you’ve had, and get an idea of how much of the entire game you’ve completed.

The third tab on the scroll shows you any certificates you’ve earned. Click the certificate on the page to enlarge it. When it's enlarged, you can print it out. The different knight certificates you can earn are:

  • Hero – For successfully completing 25 Dungeon Rescues
  • Champion – For successfully completing 25 Knight Challenges
  • Liberator – For successfully completing 25 Castle Sieges
  • Defender – For successfully completing 25 Castle Defenses

Becoming the Next King

Your power has peaked. Your experience is unmatched. It's now time to open the way into Dragonmoor! Capture Dragomont’s staff and imprison his dragon. Then the crown will be yours forever!

The final mission will require you to use all the skill and cunning you’ve learned on your earlier missions. If you can overcome the challenges and defeat Dragomont, you’ll have earned the title of King!
